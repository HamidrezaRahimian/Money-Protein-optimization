Because of the Prüfungsleistung, each of the 3 people needs a visible own responsibility, and together you must cover:

3 representation alternatives
at least 2 implemented + parameterized GA alternatives
1 naive baseline
1 local search baseline
comparison with metrics and figures
documentation of who did what clearly
Simple 3 person role split
Person 1: Problem + Representation A + Naive Baseline

Main role: the “formalization guy”

What this person does

writes the Anforderungsanalyse
writes the Problemanalyse
defines the optimization problem clearly
owns Representation Alternative A
implements the naive baseline
best choice: Random Search
helps define fitness function together with team

What to pay attention to

must clearly define:
decision vector
objective
constraints
search space
should make the problem look academic, not like “bro we shop protein”
representation must be explained properly, not just shown in code
naive baseline must be fair and comparable to GA

Best fit for this role

someone who can write okay and explain logic clearly
not the group donkey who writes random nonsense
Person 2: Representation B + Local Search

Main role: the “algorithm mechanics guy”

What this person does

owns Representation Alternative B
implements one full GA variant
implements the local search baseline
best choice: Hill Climbing or Simulated Annealing
designs mutation / crossover for this representation
helps with constraint handling

What to pay attention to

local search must fit the representation
operators must match genome structure
constraints must not be ignored like idiots
needs to explain why this representation is good or bad

Best fit for this role

someone who is decent at coding and not scared of algorithm logic
Person 3: Representation C + Evaluation + Comparison

Main role: the “evidence and results guy”

What this person does

owns Representation Alternative C
helps implement the second full GA variant
handles parameter study
handles comparison of all algorithms
creates plots, tables, and result discussion
helps structure final presentation

What to pay attention to

must compare:
GA 1
GA 2
naive
local search
should use proper metrics, not just “this one looked nicer”
needs repeated runs, because GA and random methods are stochastic
figures must be clean and understandable

Best fit for this role

someone organized, careful, and not blind when reading results
Shared things all 3 must do together

These should be decided as a group, not by one random king:

final topic wording
dataset structure
fitness function
weights / priorities
hard vs soft constraints
final decision which 2 of the 3 representations are fully implemented
final presentation story

If you do these separately, your project will look like 3 stitched-together corpses. Bad idea.

Best exact split for best grade

This is the smartest version:

Person 1

Anforderungsanalyse
Problemanalyse
Representation A
Random Search baseline

Person 2

Representation B
GA Variant 1
Local Search baseline

Person 3

Representation C
GA Variant 2
Parametrisierung
Evaluation and plots
Important grading trick

In the documentation, every chapter should clearly show:

who was hauptverantwortlich
what exactly they designed / implemented / evaluated

Otherwise the professor may think one guy worked and the other two were decorative plants 🌱

What each person must especially not forget
Person 1 must not forget

formal problem definition must be really clean

Person 2 must not forget

operators and local search must match the representation

Person 3 must not forget

evaluation must be scientific, with metrics and figures, not vibes

My honest recommendation

Choose the roles based on actual ability, not friendship fairness.

If one guy is better at writing, let him do formalization.
If one guy is better at coding, let him do GA/local search.
If one guy is better at structure and presentation, let him do evaluation.
